[Encyclopedia] (Easy) Success
Photo session
At domestic ACG offline events, you can spend one or two hundred yuan
For thirty seconds of physical close contact
Plus a Polaroid with your name handwritten by her

Two things make this act more than just consumption

First is market pricing, different people have different prices, not set arbitrarily by herself but by the market's valuation of her popularity
This means there's a public quote for how much this person is worth

Second is depth of contact, with the same person, photo session is one price, special bonus is another price, more private interactions go even higher
What you buy with higher prices isn't more time or better things, but a stronger feeling of only I can get this close to you

These two things combined, this structure itself implies a relationship rather than a transaction


Dancer plus coser plus Only exhibition performer, these three identities combined create a very special existence in China's ACG circle
She's not an idol, but she's using idol methods to do business

This isn't contradiction, it's the result of economic logic driving it
Dancing and cosplay themselves are hard to monetize, but the person herself can become a commodity
Fans are willing to pay for contact with the person
So photo sessions and Only exhibition logic naturally grafted over

The problem is, creators think they're selling photos, fans think they're cultivating a relationship
These two things were never the same thing from day one
The photo session itself creates false intimacy, fans paid money, got physical close contact
The brain interprets this as relationship rather than consumption
This is a structural problem, not Niunai's personal fault


Japanese idol industry is an interesting reference point
That system works not because fans have high quality, but because there are contracts behind it
Companies sign love-ban clauses, companies bear legal risks
Fans know the rules before entering

The domestic system took the most valuable part, pricing intimacy directly
Then threw everything else away
No company, no contract, no one backing anything up

Real life doesn't have these, no camps, no trials, no feeling of standing on the right side

This isn't a disease unique to this circle
The photo session system works not just because creators found a monetization method
But also because there's real demand waiting on the fan side
Relationships in real life are vague, unstable, unscripted
But here, rules are clear, you pay, you get exclusivity, you belong to a specific camp, your emotional investment has a clear object

**This clarity is scarce**

So they weren't deceived

When this structure breaks, like when the idol falls in love, their anger isn't at being deceived
But at that sense of structure suddenly disappearing
They want to regain control
The method is trial, taking sides, making things have right and wrong again

Creator side has no protection mechanism
Fan side has unacknowledged emotional needs
